# Productization Patterns

Use this reference when turning a prompt or workflow into a sellable product.

## Product Shapes

### Micro-SaaS

Best when the workflow repeats weekly or daily and needs saved state.

- Buyer: operator, founder, creator, agency, or niche professional.
- MVP: upload/input, structured output, history, approval, export.
- Pricing: monthly subscription or seat.

### Service-With-Software

Best when trust is high and automation is not yet reliable enough.

- Buyer: business owner or team lead.
- MVP: done-for-you output plus visible automation.
- Pricing: setup fee plus retainer.

### Chrome Extension

Best when the workflow happens inside a browser app.

- Buyer: power users trapped in existing tools.
- MVP: sidebar, extraction, action buttons, export.
- Pricing: freemium or prosumer subscription.

### Report Generator

Best when the output is a professional artifact.

- Buyer: consultant, analyst, marketer, investor, creator.
- MVP: source intake, analysis pipeline, branded export.
- Pricing: per report, credits, or subscription.

### Internal Agent

Best for teams with proprietary processes.

- Buyer: manager with budget and workflow pain.
- MVP: triage, draft, route, approve, log.
- Pricing: pilot fee and annual contract.

### Template Pack

Best when distribution matters more than software.

- Buyer: creators, operators, students, freelancers.
- MVP: prompts, examples, checklists, demo video.
- Pricing: one-time purchase or bundle.

## Productization Test

A prompt becomes a product when it has:

- Repeat frequency.
- Clear input and output.
- Buyer with budget.
- Workflow state or memory.
- Trust boundary.
- Distribution channel.

## MVP Rule

Build the smallest artifact that proves repeated use or willingness to pay. Do not build accounts, teams, dashboards, or complex integrations until the workflow is validated.

## Launch Angles

- "I turned one prompt into a sellable workflow."
- "The product is not the AI answer. It is the approval loop."
- "This tiny tool replaces 40 minutes of copy-paste work."
- "The first version is ugly because it is testing a buyer, not aesthetics."
