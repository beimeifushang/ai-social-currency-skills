# Angle Grammar

Use this reference when an AI/tech signal needs a sharper creator frame.

## Signal Archetypes

- Capability jump: a task became cheaper, faster, more reliable, or newly possible.
- Interface shift: users move from dashboards/forms/search to chat, agents, voice, camera, or ambient automation.
- Cost inversion: an expensive workflow becomes cheap enough for a new audience.
- Distribution crack: a platform, marketplace, app store, or social channel changes who can reach users.
- Trust collision: the technology works, but auditability, privacy, safety, provenance, or regulation becomes the story.
- Workflow capture: the AI tool stops being a tool and starts owning the sequence of work.

## Narrative Engines

- "The boring version is the real revolution": move from spectacle to adoption.
- "The demo is easy; the handoff is hard": separate prototype magic from production reliability.
- "The user interface is disappearing": frame agents as a UI shift, not only automation.
- "The new moat is distribution plus data exhaust": avoid model-only analysis when product channels matter.
- "This looks like a tool, but it is a labor market story": connect technical change to who gets leverage.
- "China will not copy this 1:1": localize around platforms, procurement, regulation, language, and workflows.

## Title Patterns

- "The AI feature nobody is pricing in"
- "This is not a chatbot story"
- "The dashboard era is leaking"
- "The demo works. The business model is the question."
- "AI agents are about to eat the boring work first"
- "The most important part is not the model"

## Short-Form Spine

1. Hook: name the surprising change.
2. Evidence: show one source-backed proof.
3. Tension: explain why smart people disagree.
4. Implication: name the winner, loser, or new behavior.
5. Guardrail: state what would make the take wrong.

## Source Hygiene

- Use direct sources for releases, repos, papers, docs, pricing, and benchmarks when possible.
- Treat social posts and screenshots as leads, not proof.
- Prefer "this suggests" over "this proves" when the evidence is early.
- Keep one line of visible uncertainty in the final script; it makes the creator more credible.
